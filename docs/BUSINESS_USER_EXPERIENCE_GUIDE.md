# Business User Experience Guide

Purpose: explain what a real person can do in the app, from first visit to full usage, without technical details.

## Who Uses the App

- **Renter/User**: person searching for a home and scheduling a visit.
- **Owner/Broker**: person listing homes, managing interest, and approving appointments.

## Main Promise of the Product

The app helps both sides move from interest to decision in one place:

1. discover homes
2. express interest
3. communicate through approvals and scheduling
4. meet
5. finalize
6. rate trust and quality

## User Journey (Renter/User)

### 1) Discover

The user opens the app and explores listings by:

- area/city/country
- price and home characteristics
- optional smart search features

What they feel: "I can quickly see what fits me."

### 2) Express Interest

On a listing, the user sends an inquiry.

What happens:

- the owner gets notified
- the user tracks inquiry state (pending/approved/dismissed/finalized)

What they feel: "My interest was registered and tracked."

### 3) Get Approved

If approved by owner/broker:

- user sees the listing in approved state
- user can proceed toward scheduling

What they feel: "Now I can move forward to an actual meeting."

### 4) Pick a Slot and Book

User selects available viewing time slots.

Important behavior:

- overlapping appointments are blocked to avoid double-booking
- both user and owner schedules are respected

What they feel: "I trust that this booking is real and conflict-free."

### 5) Attend and Decide

After the meeting:

- either side can move toward finalization
- finalization flow guides the decision process

What they feel: "The process is structured, not chaotic."

### 6) Rate Experience

After finalized outcomes:

- users can rate owners
- owners can rate users (with role/rule controls)

What they feel: "Good behavior is rewarded and visible."

## User Journey (Owner/Broker)

### 1) List and Manage Homes

Owner/broker creates and edits listings, including media and details.

What they feel: "I control my supply and visibility."

### 2) Review Inquiries

For each listing, owner/broker can:

- review incoming users
- approve or dismiss inquiries

What they feel: "I can qualify demand efficiently."

### 3) Set Availability

Owner/broker defines viewing windows.

What happens:

- interested/approved users are guided to booking actions
- conflict logic prevents impossible schedules

What they feel: "My calendar is protected."

### 4) Manage Notifications

Owner receives notifications for:

- new inquiries
- booking creation
- finalization events
- reminders

What they feel: "I never miss critical actions."

### 5) Finalize and Close

Owner can finalize after proper process conditions are met.

What they feel: "Deals are closed with accountability."

## Experience Principles the Product Supports

- **Clarity**: each step has a visible status.
- **Trust**: no hidden scheduling conflicts.
- **Fairness**: both sides have controlled actions.
- **Continuity**: flow goes from first click to finalization.
- **Accountability**: ratings and notifications reinforce behavior quality.

## Value for the Business

- Better conversion from listing views to real meetings.
- Fewer operational issues due to conflict-aware bookings.
- Lower support cost through clear statuses and notifications.
- Higher trust through post-flow ratings.
- Better readiness for beta due to structured lifecycle.
